Nasi Goreng

Description

Nasi goreng, Indonesia's version of fried rice, gets a sweet-savory profile from kecap manis and a big hit of umami from shrimp paste.


Ingredients

  • Long grained rice (last day's)
  • Kecap Manis
  • Dark Soy Sauce
  • Sweet Chilli Sauce
  • Peaanut oil
  • Eggs
  • Onion
  • Shrimp paste
  • Carrot
  • Chicket breat fillet
  • Prawns (peeled)
  • Green Onions

Steps

  1. Combine 2 1/2 tbsp kecap manis, 1 tbsp dark soy sauce and 1 tbsp sweet chilli sauce in a jug. Set aside.
  2. Heat a wok over high heat until hot. Add 1 teaspoon oil and swirl to coat. Add one-quarter of beaten egg and swirl to form a thin omelette. Cook for 30 seconds or until egg sets. Slide onto a board. Repeat with oil and egg, in 3 batches, to make 4 omelettes. Roll up omelettes and thinly slice crossways.
  3. Heat remaining 2 tablespoons oil in wok over high heat. Add 1 brown onion, thinly sliced, 1 tsp sambal olek (see note), 3 garlic cloves, finely chopped, 1 tsp shrimp paste and 1 carrot, peeled, finely chopped. Stir-fry for 1 minute or until aromatic. Add 1 small chicken breast fillet, chopped. Stir-fry for 1 to 2 minutes or until sealed. Add 300g green prawns, peeled, deveined, roughly chopped. Stir-fry for 2 minutes or until prawns turn pink. Add rice, soy mixture, 3 green onions, thinly sliced and 1/4 small Chinese cabbage, finely shredded. Stir-fry for 3 to 4 minutes or until rice is heated through. Toss through half the sliced omelette.
  4. Spoon nasi goreng onto serving plates. Top with remaining sliced omelette. Sprinkle with 1/4 cup fried shallots (see note). Serve with Thinly sliced red chillies, to serve.
